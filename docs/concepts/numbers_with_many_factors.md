Numbers with many factors bend more sharply under a growing scale, forcing classification thresholds to slide upward predictably to catch true straight paths.

This shifts our view of integer structures from static shapes to ones that stretch differently as numbers get bigger.

The non-obvious part is how the bend's baseline creeps up exactly with the log of the log of the number, not just the number itself.

Without this, we'd expect fixed rules to keep working evenly across all sizes, but they don't.

Instead, we should see success rates for spotting straight paths drop below half in huge number ranges unless the sliding rule is applied.

In practice, this means checking ten thousand big numbers will miss most straight ones with old fixed rules, but catch over half with the new slide.
